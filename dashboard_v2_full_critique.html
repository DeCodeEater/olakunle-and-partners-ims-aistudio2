Issues — full breakdown
Critical Recent Expenses: random rainbow colors encode nothing New problem in v2

Blue=Utilities, Orange=Repairs, Green=Legal, Purple=Marketing, Dark=Admin. None of these colors mean anything — they don't map to urgency, category type, budget status, or any real-world convention. A user's eye naturally tries to decode why Repairs is orange. It fails. Five unrelated colors in a single chart is visual noise, not information. v1's monochrome bars were actually better — at least they didn't mislead.

Fix
Use a single brand color (your navy) for all bars. Differentiation comes from the category labels, not color. Reserve color ONLY for semantic status — e.g. red if a category is over budget. Alternatively: group into 2 color categories (operational costs in navy, compliance/legal costs in a secondary tone) if there's a meaningful distinction to encode.
Critical Lease Timeline: urgency color system with no legend New problem in v2

The bars shift from red (Jun) → orange (Jul–Sep) → green (Oct–Dec). This appears to encode urgency — red = expiring soon, green = plenty of time. That's actually a smart idea. But there is no legend, no axis label, no tooltip hint, nothing explaining this system. A user seeing red bars for June without context may panic unnecessarily, or miss the convention entirely. An unlabelled encoding is worse than no encoding.

Fix
Add a 3-item legend: ● Urgent (<60 days) · ● Soon (60–90 days) · ● Planned (90+ days). Add a section subtitle: "Color indicates urgency window." This turns a confusing chart into a genuinely powerful operational tool.
Critical Mobile layout still not designed — v2 unchanged

The mobile version from v1 is unchanged. Still a vertical stack of desktop components with no bottom navigation, no tap-friendly targets, no information re-prioritisation, and charts that render too small to read. This is the biggest gap between this design and industry-standard PropTech tools.

Fix — minimum viable mobile redesign
Bottom nav bar: Dashboard · Properties · Transactions · More (44px height). On the dashboard screen: show only Revenue + Occupancy at the top. Collapse Financial Performance into a single swipeable card with 3M view as default. Put Facilitator Performance and Tenant Satisfaction behind a "Performance" tab. Lease Timeline stays visible but in a horizontal scroll. Transactions Hub becomes the last card, full-width.
Moderate Financial Performance: two charts, one ambiguous time filter

The 1W/1M/3M/6M/YD/ALL time toggle sits between the "Business Revenue Growth" chart and the "Income vs. Expense" panel. It's unclear which panel the toggle controls. Clicking "1W" would presumably change the revenue line — but does it also update gross income to ₦428.5M for just 1 week? The visual relationship is ambiguous and will cause confusion for real users.

Fix
Option A — Shared filter: Move the toggle above both panels with a connecting horizontal line or shared header that says "Period: 3M". This signals it controls everything below. Option B — Independent filters: Give each panel its own small toggle. Recommended: Option A, simpler and cleaner.
Moderate KPI cards: no hover state, no drill-down, no interaction affordance

"Total Sales Revenue ₦342.1M" is the most important number on the page. Clicking it does nothing. In Buildium, AppFolio, and Re-Leased, every headline KPI is a navigation entry point. The icon in the top-right of each card suggests interactivity but clicking it is likely also inert. KPI cards without drill-down are missed opportunities — users want to know which 18 properties, which 87 occupied units, which contracts are active.

Fix
Add a hover state: light background shift + cursor:pointer + a small "View details →" link appearing at bottom of card. On click: navigate to the relevant section pre-filtered. For Revenue: go to Financial Reports filtered to YTD. For Occupied/Vacant: go to Properties filtered by lease status. For Properties Managed: go to Properties list. This is a zero-layout-cost, high-value interaction upgrade.
Moderate Tenant Satisfaction radar still has no axis scale or scores

The pentagon shape still carries no numerical meaning. A user can see "Cleanliness is better than Amenities" from the shape, but cannot tell if Maintenance is rated 3/10 or 7/10. There's no previous-period comparison, no overall score, and no actionable threshold (e.g. "below 6 requires escalation"). This is a chart that looks good but communicates nothing specific.

Fix — consider replacing this chart entirely
Replace with a 5-row horizontal bar chart (one per dimension) showing a score out of 10, a target line at 7.5, and a trend arrow (↑↓→). This gives: exact scores, benchmark comparison, trend direction, and is far more readable. The radar chart is visually distinctive but data-poor. Keep the radar ONLY if you add axis numbers (1/5/10 rings) and a previous-quarter overlay.
Moderate Business Revenue Growth chart: no Y-axis values or data callouts

The line chart shows revenue trend from 2020 to 2024 with a nice curve, but the Y-axis has no values. "VOL: ₦3,428 AVG" is the only number — an average over 5 years shown as a single figure. A user cannot tell if 2024 is ₦200M, ₦500M, or ₦1B from the chart. The dip in 2022 could be catastrophic or trivial without scale. This makes the chart aesthetically appealing but analytically useless.

Fix
Add Y-axis gridlines with naira labels (₦100M, ₦200M, ₦300M etc). Add a tooltip on hover showing the exact monthly/quarterly value. Add a callout bubble at the most recent point showing the current value. Consider annotating the 2022 dip with a label (e.g. "Market correction") — context transforms a confusing valley into a story.
Moderate Facilitator Performance: bar chart missing totals and ranking

The grouped bar chart shows 3 facilitators across 4 quarters. It answers "who was best in Q3?" but not "who is the best facilitator overall this year?" — the most important managerial question. There's also no target line ("each facilitator should close 12 deals/quarter"), no YTD total per person, and Q4 appears to show future projections mixed with actuals without any distinction.

Fix
Add a "YTD Total" label below each facilitator name in the legend (e.g. "Sola Adebayo — 42 deals"). Add a horizontal target line across all quarters. Separate actual from projected bars using solid vs. hatched fill. Consider adding a small leaderboard table to the right of the chart showing rank, name, total deals, and % to target.
Minor Portfolio Yield "Target 9.0%" has no visual target indicator

The yield card shows 8.4% and says "Target 9.0%" but the sparkline chart doesn't show where 9.0% sits. A user has to mentally calculate "I'm 0.6% below target" — basic math, but unnecessary cognitive load. The "-0.2%" trend indicator also suggests it's moving away from target, which should be alarming, but there's no visual urgency.

Fix
Add a horizontal dashed line on the sparkline at 9.0% with label "Target". Color the 8.4% in amber (warning) since it's below target and declining. The -0.2% delta should be red, not neutral grey. This turns a static number into a clear status signal without any layout change.
Minor Live Actions in Transactions Hub: tags lack visual hierarchy

The "LEASE", "FINANCE", "DOC" tags on the right of each live action are dark navy pills that all look the same urgency. There's no way to tell if a commission payout notification is more urgent than a document upload. The timestamp format ("2HRS AGO", "10 AGO") also has inconsistent formatting (missing "D" for day on the 1D entry).

Fix
Color-code tags by type: LEASE=blue, FINANCE=green, DOC=grey. Add an urgency dot (red/amber/green) to the left of each row for action-required vs. informational. Fix timestamp consistency: use "2h ago", "1d ago", "20m ago" — lowercase, abbreviated, consistent suffix pattern.
Minor No empty states, skeleton loaders, or error handling designed

Carried over from v1 and still unresolved. Every card assumes perfect data. In production, financial data loads asynchronously, APIs fail, and some metrics may be zero. Without designed states for these conditions, the actual shipped product will show broken layouts or blank cards.

Fix
Design 3 states for every card: Loading (grey skeleton shimmer), Empty ("No data for this period — add a transaction"), Error ("Could not load — retry"). At minimum, design these for the 4 KPI cards and the Transactions Hub. This doubles your design deliverables but is non-negotiable for a production product.
Data visualisation replacements
Current: Radar chart (Tenant Satisfaction) Replace
Radars look great but are hard to read precisely and fail completely when you have fewer than 5 dimensions or any dimension is near zero.
Replace with: Horizontal score bars (5 rows, score/10, target line, trend arrow). Far more readable and actionable for a management audience.
Current: Line chart (Revenue Growth) Improve
Line chart is the right choice for trend data. But it needs Y-axis labels, gridlines, a hover tooltip, and a callout on the most recent data point to be informative.
Keep but add: Y-axis scale, period annotations, interactive tooltip, current-value callout bubble.
Current: Horizontal bars (Recent Expenses) Improve
Bar chart is correct for comparing expense categories. Values added in v2 are good. Problem is the random colors. Also missing: % of total budget, MoM change, and a total line.
Keep, fix colors to single-hue navy. Add "% of total" labels. Add a budget line. Consider a waterfall if you want to show the flow from gross income to net.
Current: Grouped bars (Facilitator Performance) Improve
Grouped bars work for comparing people across time, but they get crowded with 3 people × 4 quarters. Reading individual performance trends is difficult.
Consider: Small multiples (one chart per facilitator, same scale) or a stacked bar with YTD total labels. Add a target line per quarter. Or replace with a leaderboard table + individual sparklines.
Current: Bar chart (Lease Expiration) Keep with fix
Urgency color coding is genuinely clever and adds real operational value. Just needs the legend. Good instinct — keep it.
Add a legend (Urgent / Soon / Planned), add a subtitle explaining the color system, and optionally add a "View expiring leases" CTA that filters the Properties list.
Missing: Income vs. Expense breakdown Recommend adding
You have Gross Income ₦428.5M and Total Expenses ₦184.2M shown as numbers, but no visual that shows the relationship — where the ₦244.3M net profit sits proportionally.
Add: A simple stacked bar or waterfall chart showing Income → Expenses → Net alongside the number. A donut showing expenses as % of income is also powerful here.
Recommended layout restructure

Current layout stacks everything vertically in an undifferentiated grid. Recommend regrouping into logical zones with visual separation between executive summary, operational, and people layers.

Zone 1 — Executive summary: 4 KPI cards (clickable)
Zone 2L — Financial Performance (line chart + P&L) with unified time filter above
Zone 2R — Expenses waterfall + Budget status
Zone 3L — Facilitator leaderboard + sparklines
Zone 3R — Tenant Satisfaction score bars (replace radar)
Zone 4 — Lease Expiration Timeline (full width, urgency colors + legend)
Zone 5 — Transactions Hub (dark, full width — keep as-is)

You're fixing the right things and your instincts (urgency colors on leases, expense values, role switcher) are genuinely good. The core problems are now fewer in number but deeper in complexity: the color system needs a proper design philosophy, the mobile needs a full redesign, and the charts need to stop being decorative and start being operationally useful.
